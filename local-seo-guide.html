How Local SEO Differs from Regular SEO

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking your website in national or global organic results. Local SEO focuses on appearing in geographically-relevant results — specifically Google Maps (the Local Pack), the Google Business Profile knowledge panel, and local organic results.

Google uses three primary factors to determine local rankings: Relevance (how well your profile/site matches the search), Distance (proximity of your business to the searcher), and Prominence (how well-known and trusted your business is online).

Step 1: Claim & Fully Optimize Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important local SEO asset. A fully optimized profile gets significantly more views, clicks, and calls than incomplete ones. Complete every section:

  • Business name: Exactly as it appears on your signage — no keyword stuffing
  • Category: Choose the most specific primary category + all applicable secondary categories
  • Address & phone number: Must be identical to what appears on your website and all directories
  • Business hours: Keep updated, including special holiday hours
  • Website URL: Link directly to the most relevant page
  • Description: Write a keyword-rich, compelling 750-character description of your business
  • Photos & videos: Upload 20+ high-quality images. Businesses with photos get 35% more clicks
  • Products & services: List every service with descriptions and pricing where appropriate
  • GBP Posts: Publish weekly posts about offers, events, or updates — they appear directly in the Knowledge Panel

Step 2: NAP Consistency Across All Platforms

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone Number. Your NAP must be perfectly identical across your website, GBP, and every online directory. Even small inconsistencies (St. vs Street, Suite vs Ste) confuse Google's local algorithm and dilute your ranking signals.

Common NAP Mistake: Your GBP says "123 Main Street" but your website footer says "123 Main St." — this inconsistency weakens your local authority. Audit all your listings with Moz Local or BrightLocal.

Step 3: Build Local Citations

Local citations are mentions of your business's NAP information on other websites — even without a link. They signal to Google that your business is legitimate, established, and geographically located where you claim.

Priority citation sources:

  • Google Business Profile (most important)
  • Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, Bing Places
  • YellowPages, BBB, Angi, Foursquare
  • Industry-specific directories (TripAdvisor for restaurants, Avvo for lawyers, etc.)
  • Local chamber of commerce and business association sites
  • Local newspaper websites and community portals

Step 4: Generate & Respond to Reviews

Google reviews are a confirmed local ranking factor. More importantly, they directly influence whether a potential customer chooses you over a competitor. Strategies to generate more reviews:

  • Send a follow-up email after every purchase or service with a direct review link
  • Add a QR code at your physical location linking to your review page
  • Include a review request in your email signature
  • Ask verbally at the point of purchase when customers express satisfaction

Always respond to every review — positive and negative. Google uses your response rate as a prominence signal, and potential customers read your responses.

Step 5: Optimize Your Website for Local SEO

Your website must support your GBP with strong local on-page signals:

  • Include your NAP in the footer of every page (in Schema markup too)
  • Create a dedicated Contact page with your full address, phone, and embedded Google Map
  • Use local keywords naturally in page titles, headings, and body content (e.g., "SEO agency in Chicago")
  • Implement LocalBusiness schema markup with your address, phone, hours, and geo-coordinates
  • Create location-specific pages if you serve multiple cities
  • Embed a Google Map on your Contact page

Step 6: Build Local Backlinks

Local backlinks from geographically relevant websites signal local authority to Google. Target:

  • Local newspaper coverage and press releases
  • Local business association membership pages
  • Sponsorship of local events, sports teams, or charities
  • Partnerships with complementary local businesses
  • Guest posts on local community blogs and websites
How long does local SEO take to show results?
Many clients see improvements in GBP visibility within 4–8 weeks of optimization and citation building. Breaking into the local 3-pack typically takes 2–6 months depending on competition level in your area and niche.
Can I rank in cities where I don't have a physical location?
For Google Maps/3-pack results, a verified physical address in that city is generally required. However, you can rank in local organic results for other cities through targeted content and off-page authority. Service area businesses can set service areas in GBP.
Do I need local SEO if I have a website that ranks organically?
Yes. Local 3-pack results appear above organic results on both mobile and desktop. Many users never scroll to the organic results when they see the Maps pack. Local and organic SEO serve different ranking systems and must be optimized separately.
