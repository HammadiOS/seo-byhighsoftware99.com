Ecommerce Site Architecture

A logical site architecture is critical for ecommerce. Every product should be reachable in 3 clicks or fewer from the homepage. A clean hierarchy looks like:

  • Homepage → Category → Subcategory → Product
  • Use breadcrumb navigation on every page (and breadcrumb schema markup)
  • Create a flat structure where possible — deep nesting buries pages from crawlers
  • Interlink related products and categories to distribute PageRank

Category Page Optimization

Category pages are your highest-value pages for transactional keywords like "buy running shoes" or "women's winter coats." They often have the highest authority but are frequently neglected. Optimize each category page with:

  • A unique H1 targeting the primary category keyword
  • 200–400 words of unique introductory content above the product grid
  • Unique meta title and description for every category
  • Internal links to subcategories and related content
  • Breadcrumb navigation with schema markup
  • Canonical tags on paginated pages (page 2, 3, etc.)

Product Page SEO

Product pages target high-intent, purchase-ready searchers. Every product page must be uniquely optimized:

1

Unique Product Title & H1

Include the primary keyword (product name + key attribute). Example: "Nike Air Max 270 Men's Running Shoes - Black/White" not just "Nike Air Max".

2

Unique Product Description

Never use manufacturer descriptions — they create duplicate content across hundreds of stores. Write unique, benefit-focused descriptions with relevant keywords naturally included.

3

Product Schema Markup

Implement Product schema with Name, Description, Image, Brand, SKU, Price, Availability, and AggregateRating. This enables rich snippets showing price and availability directly in SERPs.

4

Customer Reviews

User-generated content from reviews adds unique, keyword-rich content to product pages automatically. Encourage reviews with post-purchase email sequences.

5

Image Optimization

Compress all product images, use descriptive file names (nike-air-max-270-black.webp), and write unique, keyword-rich alt text for every image angle.

Handling Faceted Navigation

Faceted navigation (filter by color, size, price) creates thousands of URL combinations that can cause duplicate content and crawl budget waste. Solutions:

  • Use canonical tags pointing faceted URLs to the main category page
  • Block parameter-generated URLs in robots.txt (only if they have no unique value)
  • Implement noindex on filtered pages that don't target specific keywords
  • Create separate SEO-optimized pages for high-value filter combinations (e.g., "red Nike running shoes")

Crawl Budget Alert: Large ecommerce stores with faceted navigation can have Googlebot wasting its entire crawl budget on parameter URLs, leaving important product pages undiscovered. Technical ecommerce SEO is critical for stores with 1,000+ products.

Ecommerce Keyword Strategy

Ecommerce keywords fall into three tiers — target all three for maximum coverage:

  • Transactional product keywords: "buy [product name]," "[product] price," "[product] for sale" → Target on product pages
  • Category keywords: "women's running shoes," "organic skincare products" → Target on category pages
  • Informational keywords: "how to choose running shoes," "best skincare routine" → Target with blog content that links to products

Ecommerce Technical SEO Priorities

  • Page speed: A 1-second delay in load time reduces conversions by 7%. Image compression is usually the biggest win.
  • XML sitemap: Include only canonical, indexable product and category pages — exclude parameter URLs, out-of-stock pages (if not seasonal), and thin content.
  • Out-of-stock products: Keep the URL live with a 200 status, show related products, and add the product back when restocked. Only 404 discontinued products with no similar replacement.
  • Pagination: Use proper rel="next/prev" signals and ensure all pages in a series are discoverable and canonicalized.
Is Shopify good for SEO?
Shopify is a solid ecommerce platform for SEO with automatic sitemaps, canonical tags, and mobile-responsive themes. Its main SEO limitations include URL structure (forced /products/ and /collections/ prefixes) and limited control over technical elements. With proper optimization, Shopify stores can rank very competitively.
How do I rank for product keywords vs. Amazon?
Amazon dominates many generic product keywords. Focus on specific long-tail product keywords, brand + model combinations, and comparison queries. Build niche topical authority through blog content, and earn brand-specific backlinks. Your unique brand story is an advantage Amazon can't replicate.
Should I index out-of-stock product pages?
Generally yes, especially for seasonal or cyclical products. Keep them indexed with rich content, add structured data marking them as "OutOfStock," show similar products, and capture email signups for restock notifications. This preserves link equity and rankings for when the product returns.
